ABOUT ME

A Little Helpful History

It's true. Like most kids, I could often be seen in my youth tumbling over cardboard boxes in my backyard, pretending to fly my spacship from planet to planet, saving mankind from eminent destruction. In my world, the sky was definitely NOT the limit. When I grew up, and began preparations for my collegiate career, family and freinds began to barrade me with questions of my plans and aspirations.

I asked them, "Why? Do you think I've changed?"

In middle and high school, I went out of my way to take every engineering, math, and physics course there was to take. I joined robotics clubs, science olympiad, the mathletes, and even took a few college level courses when I had the time. I just couldn't get enough of it. Now, three years deep in my Software Engineering degree, I'm still striving.
Recently, I've been studying Machine Learning with Professor Shaoen Wu at Ball State to understand computers and robotics even more, and working part time at a company to help build websites and apps for local businesses.

The end goal is the same as it's always been. Find a way to space.

EXPERIENCE

How Do I Know What I'm Doing?

In the last few years, I've been doing the absolute best that I can to prepare myself for this future dream career of mine. In the classroom, I was involved in Indiana's 'Project Lead The Way' (PLTW) courses for 5 years, gaining skills in the principals of engineering and mechanical design; I was also a state finalist in the Indiana LEGO Robotics tournament a few years ago, before my runner-up finish in the 80/20 Engineering Challenge a few years later.

In the workforce, my technology experience runs a bit deeper. I am currently an IT Intern for the Catholic Diocese of Fort Wayne / South Bend, and will soon begin another internship with a biomedical engineering company in Warsaw, Indiana.

Not to mention the year-long machine learning project mentioned above, and the fun things I do on the side to further my education! For me, the learning never stops!
